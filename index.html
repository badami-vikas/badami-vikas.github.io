Transitional Thinking
  1. The steam engine is a better source of power.
  2. By replacing water wheels with steam engines, manufacturers powered existing mills more reliably.
  3. By preserving existing workflows, they built better mills.
Transformational Thinking
  1. The steam engine is a new way to organize production.
  2. By centralizing workers and machines around steam power, manufacturers coordinated specialized production at unprecedented scale.
  3. By redesigning work around centralized production, they created the factory system.

# Alt homepage: per-section design solutions

Per the non-negotiable constraint in `creative-direction.md`, each section was designed emotion-first. This file records the solved communication problem BEFORE any copy or UI existed. Implementation: `index.html` on branch `alt/story-driven-home`.

## 1. The Shift
- **Emotion in:** mild skepticism ("another AI pitch").
- **Emotion out:** uneasy recognition ("this exact pattern has happened four times, and it's happening again").
- **Transformation:** AI reframed from technology trend to platform shift; the visitor notices, by themselves, that every era begins by recreating the previous era, and that gains arrive only when the organization reorganizes.
- **Medium:** interactive era timeline (click through Steam → Electricity → Computer → Internet → AI). Each era shows the same two-beat structure: "first, the old world recreated" / "then, the organization reorganized." The AI era's second beat is intentionally blank: a "?" the visitor completes.
- **Curiosity hook out:** "So what does an AI-native organization actually look like?"

## 2. AI-native Organization
- **Emotion in:** curiosity.
- **Emotion out:** wonder ("the org chart itself is what changes").
- **Transformation:** from "we'll keep the org and add AI" to "knowledge flow, decision paths, meetings, and management itself reorganize."
- **Medium:** a living org illustration. One element transforms at a time, chosen by the visitor (Knowledge / Decisions / Meetings / Managers). Before and after are always comparable. Nothing is explained; everything is observed.
- **Hook out:** "Something has to hold all of this up. What?"

## 3. Business AI Infrastructure
- **Emotion in:** confidence in a wrong assumption (infrastructure = servers).
- **Emotion out:** humility and insight (technology is the thinnest layer).
- **Transformation:** leadership, governance, knowledge, decision-making, trust, culture, and measurement ARE the infrastructure; technology merely enables them.
- **Medium:** cross-section of a building. Starts showing only the server-room layer; each press of "look deeper" reveals another invisible floor until technology is visibly one small layer of a much larger system.
- **Hook out:** "Infrastructure needs stewards. Who manages a workforce that never sleeps?"

## 4. Managing AI
- **Emotion in:** control anxiety ("who's watching this thing?").
- **Emotion out:** calm stewardship ("this is management, and I already know how to manage").
- **Transformation:** managing AI resembles managing people, not configuring software: a continuous discipline of quality, trust, memory, boundaries, learning.
- **Medium:** interactive stewardship console. Selecting a duty shows what a steward actually does this week, phrased exactly like people management.
- **Hook out:** "This discipline barely exists yet. How does an organization grow into it?"

## 5. Consulting
- **Emotion in:** overwhelm ("transformation projects fail").
- **Emotion out:** confidence ("organizations grow, capability by capability").
- **Transformation:** from "install a transformation" to "mature through unlocked capabilities."
- **Medium:** a journey path, not a roadmap. Five milestones, each a capability the organization keeps forever, each unlocking the next.
- **Hook out:** "And who walks beside your people while they grow?"

## 6. Platform (Zazoo appears here, as the conclusion)
- **Emotion in:** intellectual conviction.
- **Emotion out:** warmth, desire ("I want one beside me").
- **Transformation:** from "AI is a tool I operate" to "a companion lives alongside my work."
- **Medium:** character moment. Aeva working, small caring speech bubbles, behavior not capability.
- **Hook out:** "A companion multiplies you. So what remains uniquely yours?"

## 7. Training
- **Emotion in:** quiet replacement fear.
- **Emotion out:** aspiration ("AI makes my leadership MORE valuable").
- **Transformation:** the more capable the companions, the more valuable judgment, taste, trust, direction, and care become.
- **Medium:** reveal cards: "what becomes uniquely human." Each card is a one-sentence story, not a curriculum item.
- **Close:** Zazoo as conclusion + single CTA.

Global: a running documentary thread; every section ends with the question the next answers. Zazoo does not appear (name, mascot, or product) before section 6, except the site nav.

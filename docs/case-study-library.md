# Case Study Library

Real, citable cases to forward the website's arguments. Organized by the argument they support, with the section where each slots best. Pick freely; each entry is one paragraph of usable narrative plus its source.

---

## Argument 1: Platform shifts redesign organizations (Section 1, The Shift)

**The Dynamo (Paul David, Stanford).** Electricity reached factories in the 1890s, yet productivity barely moved for ~30 years. Why: owners unbolted the steam engine and bolted a dynamo in its place, keeping the old central-shaft factory. Gains arrived only in the 1920s, when "unit drive" (a motor per machine) let factories be redesigned around workflow instead of around the power source. The canonical proof that the technology is the fast part; the organization is the slow part. Source: [The Dynamo and the Computer (PDF)](http://www.dklevine.com/archive/refs4115.pdf).

**Ford's Highland Park (1913).** The assembly line cut chassis assembly from about 12.5 hours to about 93 minutes. The innovation wasn't a machine; it was a reorganization of work made possible by electric unit drive. New organizational model, not new tool. Source: [Ford, The Moving Assembly Line](https://corporate.ford.com/articles/history/moving-assembly-line.html).

**The computer's "productivity paradox" (Solow, 1987).** "You can see the computer age everywhere but in the productivity statistics." Firms computerized typing pools and filing cabinets: paperless paper. Returns showed up in the 1990s only in firms that reorganized processes around information flow (Brynjolfsson & Hitt's research). Source: [Beyond Computation, JEP 2000](https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.14.4.23).

**Blockbuster vs Netflix.** Blockbuster put its catalogue online (the old world, recreated). Netflix rebuilt the organization around the new distribution logic: subscriptions, then streaming, then data-driven commissioning. Same technology available to both; different organizational conclusion. Source: [Harvard Business School, Blockbuster case background](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=31457).

**Encyclopædia Britannica vs Wikipedia.** The internet-native organization wasn't a better publisher; it was a different organizational species (open contribution, continuous revision). Print set ended 2012 after 244 years. Source: [Britannica announcement coverage, NYT](https://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/14/books/britannica-stops-printing-print-edition.html).

## Argument 2: The organization itself changes, not just workflows (Section 2, AI-native Organization)

**Haier's RenDanHeYi.** The world's largest appliance maker dissolved its middle-management bureaucracy into ~4,000 self-managing micro-enterprises facing users directly. Proof that a large industrial company can change its organizational physics, not just its tools. Source: [Corporate Rebels on RenDanHeYi](https://www.corporate-rebels.com/blog/haier) / [HBR: The End of Bureaucracy](https://hbr.org/2018/11/the-end-of-bureaucracy).

**Buurtzorg.** Dutch home-care nonprofit: ~10,000 nurses in self-managing teams of 10-12, ~50 back-office staff, no managers in the classic sense. Repeatedly rated best employer; strong outcomes at lower cost. The "manager as information router" role was designed out. Source: [Buurtzorg model](https://www.buurtzorg.com/about-us/buurtzorgmodel/).

**Amazon's two-pizza teams and working backwards.** Organizational primitives (small autonomous teams, narrative memos, single-threaded owners) designed so decision paths stay short at scale. The org design IS the strategy. Source: [Working Backwards (Bryar & Carr)](https://www.workingbackwards.com/).

**Moderna: 750+ custom GPTs.** Not one chatbot: hundreds of task-shaped AI assistants created largely by employees themselves (40% of weekly active users built their own), legal at 100% adoption. What "the organization reorganizes around AI" looks like in its first inning. Source: [OpenAI, Moderna case](https://openai.com/index/moderna/).

**Klarna's AI assistant.** In its first month it handled two-thirds of customer-service chats (2.3M conversations, 23 markets, 35 languages), the estimated work of 700 agents, resolution 11 min → under 2 min. Also a governance parable: Klarna later re-hired for human service quality, showing the redesign is a judgment problem, not a headcount problem. Sources: [Klarna press](https://www.klarna.com/international/press/klarna-ai-assistant-handles-two-thirds-of-customer-service-chats-in-its-first-month/), [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/klarna/).

## Argument 3: Infrastructure is more than technology (Section 3)

**Toyota Production System.** Toyota's advantage was never machines; competitors toured the plants freely and still couldn't copy it, because the infrastructure was standard work, andon culture, and supplier trust. "Operating system" as social architecture. Source: [Toyota, TPS](https://global.toyota/en/company/vision-and-philosophy/production-system/).

**Aviation's safety infrastructure.** Commercial flying became the safest transport not through better engines alone but through checklists, crew resource management, blameless incident review, and clear authority gradients: governance as the enabling layer. Source: [Gawande, The Checklist Manifesto background, New Yorker](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2007/12/10/the-checklist).

**Google's Project Aristotle.** Five years of data across 180+ teams: the top predictor of team performance was psychological safety, not seniority, co-location, or tooling. Trust is measurable infrastructure. Source: [re:Work, Project Aristotle](https://rework.withgoogle.com/print/guides/5721312655835136/).

**SRE error budgets.** Google's reliability practice turns "how much risk may we take?" into an explicit, negotiated budget between product and operations: measurement + governance as the thing that makes speed safe. Source: [Google SRE Book, Embracing Risk](https://sre.google/sre-book/embracing-risk/).

## Argument 4: Managing AI is a management discipline (Section 4)

**JPMorgan COIN.** Contract-review AI that absorbed ~360,000 lawyer-hours a year of document review, run under bank-grade controls, model governance, and audit. AI at scale looks like supervised delegation, not installed software. Source: [Bloomberg, JPMorgan COIN](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-02-28/jpmorgan-marshals-an-army-of-developers-to-automate-high-finance).

**GitHub Copilot RCT.** Controlled experiment: developers with Copilot completed the task ~55% faster. But org-level studies (e.g. DORA) show gains only materialize where review, testing, and delivery practices absorb the new speed: the bottleneck moves to management systems. Sources: [GitHub research](https://github.blog/news-insights/research/research-quantifying-github-copilots-impact-on-developer-productivity-and-happiness/), [DORA reports](https://dora.dev/research/).

**Air Canada chatbot ruling (2024).** A tribunal held the airline liable for its chatbot's wrong advice: "the chatbot did it" is not a defense. The case for boundaries, evals, and accountability as ongoing management duties. Source: [CBC coverage](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/british-columbia/air-canada-chatbot-lawsuit-1.7116416).

**Knight Capital (2012).** $440M lost in 45 minutes from an unsupervised deployment of automated trading logic. The pre-AI proof that autonomous systems without operational stewardship are a balance-sheet risk. Source: [SEC order](https://www.sec.gov/litigation/admin/2013/34-70694.pdf).

## Argument 5: Transformation is organizational, not technical (Section 5, Consulting)

**GE's digital transformation failure.** Billions into Predix and GE Digital with a tool-first, center-out approach; the industrial organization never reorganized around it. Cautionary mirror image of Section 1. Source: [HBR, GE's digital struggles](https://hbr.org/2018/07/ges-fall-shows-why-digital-transformation-is-a-marathon).

**Microsoft's culture turnaround.** Nadella's reframing ("know-it-all" → "learn-it-all", growth mindset) preceded and enabled the cloud/AI pivot; the technology strategy rode on an organizational-psychology change. Source: [Hit Refresh, Nadella](https://news.microsoft.com/hitrefresh/).

**AT&T Future Ready.** Facing the network-software shift, AT&T spent ~$1B reskilling ~100,000 employees rather than replacing them: transformation as workforce maturation, a journey with unlocked capabilities. Source: [HBR, AT&T's Talent Overhaul](https://hbr.org/2016/10/atts-talent-overhaul).

**Shopify's AI memo (2025).** CEO Tobi Lütke made "reflexive AI usage" a baseline expectation and required teams to prove AI can't do a job before asking for headcount: an organization changing its operating covenant, not buying a tool. Source: [Tobi Lütke memo, X](https://x.com/tobi/status/1909251946235437514).

## Argument 6: Companionship beats tooling (Section 6, Platform)

**Duolingo's streak psychology.** Retention driven by relationship mechanics (streaks, the owl's persona) rather than feature count: emotional design as product moat. Source: [Duolingo blog on streaks](https://blog.duolingo.com/how-duolingo-streak-builds-habit/).

**Clippy vs modern companions.** Microsoft's Clippy failed by interrupting without context or memory; its descendants succeed by being ambient, contextual, and personal. The lesson: presence without care is annoyance. Source: [Stanford HCI retrospective on Clippy, CHI literature](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/632716.632718).

## Argument 7: Leadership becomes more valuable (Section 7, Training)

**Chess advanced ("centaur") era.** After Deep Blue, human+machine teams beat both grandmasters and supercomputers for years; the differentiator was the human's process for directing the machine. Judgment as the multiplier. Source: [Kasparov, The Chess Master and the Computer, NYRB](https://www.nybooks.com/articles/2010/02/11/the-chess-master-and-the-computer/).

**BCG GPT-4 field experiment (Harvard/Wharton/MIT).** 758 consultants: AI lifted average task quality ~40%, lifted the bottom performers most, but degraded results when used outside its "jagged frontier": the skill that mattered was knowing when to trust it. Exactly the leadership curriculum. Source: [Navigating the Jagged Frontier, SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4573321).

**Ikea's call-center reskilling.** As AI (Billie) absorbed routine calls, IKEA retrained 8,500 call-center workers into interior-design advisors: the human work moved up to taste, empathy, and judgment. Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/ikea-bets-remote-interior-design-ai-chatbot-billie-frees-up-call-centre-workers-2023-06-12/).
